    <p class="justified">
        We investigate complex dynamical systems to uncover general principles governing irreversibility, transport, and fluctuations far from equilibrium. Our work focuses on speed limits on diffusion, transport, and thermalization, emerging from both deterministic and stochastic dynamics, and on how these limits are organized by the geometry of irreversibility encoded in force structure and dynamical correlations. We study these ideas across driven, disordered, and many-body systems, with a growing emphasis on living systems, where non-equilibrium dynamics is essential for function and adaptability. Our approach is theoretical, rooted in mathematics, physics, and computation, and guided by experimentally relevant phenomena where dynamics plays a central role.
    </p>
